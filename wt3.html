Co-op Work Term 3: Instructional and Technical Support Assistant

My experience working as an Instructional and Technical Support Assistant at the University of Guelph's Office of Teaching and Learning.

About the Office of Teaching and Learning

For my third co-op work term, and my first remote work experience, I had the opportunity to work for the Office of Teaching and Learning at the University of Guelph. The team that represents this office aims to advance the university’s pedagogy practices through a variety of projects, including research work, informational webinars, training modules for instructors, and more.

What did my role consist of?

I was involved in several projects during my work term. Some of them were:

  • Post-webinar production: I was responsible for editing the office’s webinar series on teaching practices. After editing, I would upload them to YouTube, add closed captioning, and distribute the link to the webinar’s attendees. I had previous experience editing videos outside of school, though I became familiar with new editing software, namely, WeVideo and iMovie.

  • Website layout re-design: I assisted in the process of redesigning the Office of Teaching and Learning’s website. This involved researching other websites with effective layouts, taking ideas from different sources and piecing them together for inspiration. Through this process, I was able to exercise my critical and creative thinking skills.

  • Research snapshots: I read research papers on teaching and learning practices, gathered information that was important for the reader to know, and used Clear Language Writing to create a summary. I was taught how to use Clear Language Writing by my supervisor, and I believe this skill will come in handy in any work that involves writing in the future. This project involved a high level of critical analysis and reflection, which I believe is always important in the field of computer science, in programming and design.

Overall, working on these projects simultaneously helped me strengthen my organization, communication, and time management skills. I was required to organize my calendar wisely to meet deadlines, communicate and synchronize with my team members, and learn how to prioritize certain tasks over others.

My work term goals

I had three main goals while working as an Instructional and Technical Support Assistant:

  1. Personal Organization and Time Management
    In my co-op position with the Office of Teaching and Learning, I worked on multiple projects simultaneously. I felt that in order for me to make meaningful contributions to the projects, I should work on further improving my organization and time management skills. These skills are vital in countless areas and will help me in the rest of my studies in computer science, as I often have to work on multiple assignments and projects at once to meet deadlines.

    Reflection:
    At the beginning of my work term, it was a little overwhelming having several projects to focus on, each with their own objectives. Shortly after, I started using my calendar and setting deadlines for myself. I also kept a notes folder on my desktop with separate pages outlining the tasks that I need to complete for each project. I found these techniques to be very effective, as I was hitting my deadlines sooner than expected, and was praised by my supervisors for my efforts.

  2. Inquiry and Analysis
    As an Instructional and Technical Support Assistant, I anticipated that some project work would require a degree of critical thinking and analysis. Specifically, in one of the projects, I had to analyze information on our team’s website, inquire on how it can be improved, compare it to other websites, and work on solutions through this process. I wanted to use this opportunity to improve my inquiry and analysis skills.

    Reflection:
    In the website redesign project, I spent a lot of time reflecting and asking what makes a website easy to navigate. My team members and I shared and combined our ideas and analyzed further. In the future if I decide to pursue web development, through personal projects or professional work, the inquiry and analysis skills I developed through this project will come in handy.

  3. Teamwork
    In one of the projects, I worked closely with my supervisor and co-op teammate to reformat and re-organize an educational training module. I wanted to focus on exercising my teamwork and collaboration skills, so that my contributions to the project can flow smoothly with my team members’. I also recognize the importance of being able to co-ordinate with a team in programming assignments and software development jobs.

    Reflection:
    To make our teamwork as cohesive as possible, I documented all my project work for my team members to see. I frequently checked in with my team and considered how we can combine our progress. I made note of any questions as they arose and asked my supervisor for clarification often. Overall, the team’s progress flowed smoothly, and we hit deadlines without trouble. Through this project, I strengthened my teamwork skills, and got a better understanding of the importance of communication in coordinating with a team.

Thank you!
